Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol (TCP/IP)

TCP/IP is the fundamental suite of communication protocols used to connect hosts on the internet. It is often referred to as the "language" of the internet, ensuring that data sent from one computer arrives correctly at another.

How it Works:

  • TCP (Transmission Control Protocol): Breaks data into small packets before they are sent and reassembles them when they arrive. It ensures no data is lost during the move.
  • IP (Internet Protocol): Acts like a GPS for the data packets. it addresses and routes the packets so they know exactly which computer to go to.

Together, they allow different types of computers (like a Mac talking to a PC) to communicate seamlessly across the globe.
